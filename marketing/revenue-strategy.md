# Anjoy Space — Revenue Strategy

> How to grow revenue, prioritized and honest. Living doc. Last updated: 2026-07-18.

## The key question: demand- or supply-constrained?
Anjoy's own signals — retreats **fully booked**, **~94% return rate** — suggest the business is
**supply-constrained** (it sells out). If true, **more marketing is NOT the biggest lever** (it just
creates waitlists). The money is in **price, capacity, and revenue lines not capped by physical retreats.**

⚠️ **Confirm first:** some of those figures live on the `/retreat-center` vision page and may be partly
aspirational. The whole priority order pivots on whether Anjoy is truly demand- or supply-constrained.

## Levers, in priority order

### 🟢 Immediate / low effort
1. **Raise prices.** Sell out every time = underpriced. Fastest revenue, zero added cost. Even +10–15%
   likely still sells out. Add a **premium tier** (best room / private session / extra perks).
2. **Online booking + deposit.** Cuts leakage between "interested" and "paid"; a **deposit + cancellation
   policy** protects against no-shows. Also the #1 platform gap vs Yoga Barn / Vikasa.
3. **Upsells / add-ons per booking** — extra nights, private sessions, room upgrades, transfers, excursions.
   Raises average order value with existing customers.
4. **Gift cards** — cash upfront + acquires *new* customers (recipients). Both competitors sell them.
5. **Formalize referral** — 94% return but no program capturing it. "Bring a friend → both save." Cheapest
   new-customer channel.

### 🟡 Bigger — breaks the physical-retreat ceiling (real growth)
6. **Online academy / on-demand + subscription.** ⭐ The **uncapped** lever — scales beyond dates/seats,
   Ani already teaches online. Recurring revenue + global reach. Start small (a few recorded courses / monthly membership).
7. **Teacher Training (YTT).** High-ticket ($2–4k), high-margin, builds authority — and trains teachers who
   can later lead/host retreats (grows supply too). The engine for both Vikasa and Yoga Barn.
8. **More supply** — more dates, more teachers (Yulia/Katya leading their own), and the **Lori/Gavar center**.
   Converts waitlist demand into revenue.

### 🔵 Strategic — for the center
9. **Retreat-hosting model** — let other teachers run retreats at the center for a commission. Fills the
   calendar without Anjoy's labor; de-risks the center financially. (See competitor-analysis.md.)
10. **Corporate / team retreats** — page exists; B2B is high-value, less price-sensitive. Actively sell it.

## What to do first (given current stage: gift-for-Ani, center still a vision)
Near-term revenue stack, all doable now, all high-ROI:
**raise prices → booking + deposit → upsells + gift cards → referral program.**

Biggest future unlock: the **online academy** — the one thing that grows revenue without more of Ani's
physical time.

## Revenue math (mental model)
Revenue = customers × value-per-booking × frequency. Levers map to:
- **value-per-booking:** price, premium tiers, upsells, add-ons
- **frequency / LTV:** referral, loyalty, membership, academy, rebooking
- **customers:** referral, corporate, aggregators, SEO/IG (only worth pushing once supply can absorb it)
- **new lines (uncapped by seats):** academy, YTT, gift cards, hosting commission
